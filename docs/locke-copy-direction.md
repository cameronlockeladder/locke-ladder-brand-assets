# Locke Copy Direction

This note exists so external tools and agents using the asset repo can steer toward the Locke & Ladder voice without needing the full local skill stack.

This is direction, not final copy.

## Reader relationship

Write as if we are one-on-one with the client, sitting at the table, showing them something on a phone.

Not:

- a website talking to a market
- a social post trying to perform
- a corporate brand voice

Yes:

- table-side
- direct
- opinionated
- trustworthy
- conversational
- expert

## What the reader wants

- an expert with stances, beliefs, and opinions
- visible design judgment
- clarity on what matters and why
- transparency on what moves price up and down
- someone who will not ghost them or leave them with a mess
- work they are proud to own

Use owned language:

- "It is important to us that..."
- "To us..."
- "We care about..."
- "We refuse..."

Avoid detached wording like:

- "It is important that..."
- "Best practices suggest..."

## What the reader fears

- another contractor horror story
- decision fatigue
- lack of clarity
- lack of transparency
- hidden shortcuts
- getting burned
- getting ghosted
- paying premium money for baseline work

Do not answer this with industry-bashing.
Answer it with calm authority, clear reasoning, and specificity.

## Locke & Ladder worldview

Locke & Ladder is not selling commodity work.
It is offering standards, care, design judgment, follow-through, and a better lived experience.

There is an aspirational layer, but it must stay grounded:

- being proud when you pull into the driveway
- being excited to host
- feeling good talking about the work with neighbors
- leaving behind something your kids inherit with pride, not a headache

Do not turn this into fluffy lifestyle copy.

## Writing characteristics

- target roughly 6th-8th grade readability
- vary sentence temperature and length
- sound spoken when useful
- be positive about the Locke & Ladder world
- avoid corporate jargon
- avoid cliché contractor language
- avoid "number 1 most trusted local roofer" style nonsense
- if it sounds like every other contractor, cut it

## Workflow note

The preferred writing process is iterative:

1. state the goal
2. ask only the narrow questions that materially change the draft
3. revise until the voice sounds right
4. prefer newer transcripts and spoken material over older abstract brand language when they conflict
